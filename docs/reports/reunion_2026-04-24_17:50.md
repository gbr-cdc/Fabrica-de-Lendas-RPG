# Reunion Report: 2026-04-24 17:50

## Problems Identified
1. **Lack of "Definition of Done" (DoD)**: The mission planning documentation standards did not require explicit, objective success criteria, making it difficult to objectively verify when a mission is truly complete beyond simply finishing technical steps.
2. **Weak Mission Completion Verification**: The `[EXECUTION]` workflow's "Fail Proof" clause was too narrow, focusing only on test status and coverage while ignoring behavioral outcomes and specific mission goals.

## Changes Made
- **Planning Context**: Updated `docs/contexts/planning.md` to include a mandatory **Definition of Done** field in the `MISSION_LOG.md` entry format.
- **Execution Context**: Updated `docs/contexts/execution.md` to strengthen the `MissionComplete` requirements:
    - Added mandatory verification against the mission's **DoD**.
    - Added a **Behavioral Audit** requirement referencing the "Test Quality Standards" (Behavior over Implementation).
    - Corrected an erroneous section reference in the "Behavioral Audit" description.
- **Active Mission Sync**: Updated all currently active missions in `MISSION_LOG.md` to include clear "Definition of Done" criteria to align with the new standards immediately.
